    Introduction

    Our brains subconsciously solve various problems we face every day by
    using objects in ways that they were not designed for. For example, it could be as simple as placing a coat on the backs of our chair when we arrive at University or work, or maybe we use a pen or ruler to act as a bookmark.     This website focuses on how we use affordance to help solve common
    problems that occur from residing in smaller living spaces. This includes: items we substitute in place of something else, items that end up becoming useful
    for lots of different jobs or even items that have been specifically designed to
    be compact, convenient or efficient.     The goal of the project is to present all the ingenious ways we use affordance
    to interact with objects around our living spaces.
